Dinner

I find myself here again, sitting in the latest high end, trendy restaurant that I cannot afford, among clusters of hip New Yorkers who waited weeks for this reservation. And I am listening. Listening as I have done for the last ten years. This time it’s the familiar story of how you quit another perfectly good job because:

a) you messed up
b) you can’t get along with other people
c) you’re having a major personal crisis
d) all of the above

Actually, all of your stories, whether related to work or relationships, have the same multiple-choice ending. And I listen because that’s what friends do. Especially your friends, who are few and far-between. I listen to every painstaking detail of every moment. I nod in agreement as I sip my $20 cocktail. I barely say anything and when I do, it goes unnoticed.

Dinner arrives, a plate of something lovely yet forgettable. And the listening continues. I really do want to say, “I completely disagree with the way you handled the situation, you messed up, what’s wrong with you?” But I can’t bring myself to confront you in your vulnerable state. And therein lies the problem: you are always in a vulnerable state. It takes a lot of emotional and mental strength to be your friend. I am not strong. In fact, eating this dinner I feel weak, almost nauseous. Perhaps I am the vulnerable one and you the strong one. You must be strong in some sense to cope with your self-made crises. Yes, that’s it! I am too vulnerable to deal with you. I shouldn’t be here. I will not allow myself to be put in this position again. The check arrives. It sickens me that I have to lay down my credit card because it’s my “turn” but I am comforted by the realization that this will be the last time.
